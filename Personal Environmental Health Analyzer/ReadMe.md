# Personal Environmental Health Analyzer

## 📊 Real Data Used (sourced live):
Lagos current AQI: 53 (Moderate), last recorded June 23, 2026 — with a 24-hour range of 25 (Good) to 90 (Moderate) AQI https://www.aqi.in/us/dashboard/nigeria
Warri ranks as Nigeria's most polluted city at AQI ~78 (Moderate, 4× above standard), with Port Harcourt, Calabar, Lagos, Ikeja, and Abuja filling out the national ranking AQI https://www.aqi.in/us/dashboard/nigeria
Research study (2021–2023) across Lagos, Abuja, Anyigba, Osogbo and Benin City confirmed PM2.5 AQI averages of 98, 108, 47, 85, and 114 respectively Nsps https://journal.nsps.org.ng/index.php/jnsps/article/download/2120/337
Nigeria's water supply has documented 91% E. Coli contamination prevalence, with urban coastal settlements in Lagos particularly affected UNEP GRID https://dicf.unepgrid.ch/nigeria/pollution

## What's interactive:

Gauge updates live when you click any city card
6 Charts — AQI comparison, pollutant selector (PM2.5/PM10/NO₂/SO₂/O₃/CO), PM10, AQI distribution doughnut, water quality bar, and city ranking
Filters — AQI range sliders, health risk chips (All / Good / Moderate / Poor), pollutant tabs
City Comparison — pick any two cities from the Compare dropdown to see a side-by-side strip
10 clickable city cards — each updates the full analysis, report card, score rings, and recommendations
Risk indicators — 🟢 Low / 🟡 Moderate / 🔴 High across all 12 health dimensions

# The Core Message 
The air Nigerians breathe and the water they use are not yet safe enough. The numbers show this clearly, and the effects are visible in everyday life in tired bodies, persistent coughs, thinning hair, dry skin, and hospital bills. But the situation is not hopeless, and it is not fixed. With the right information, every person can make daily choices that reduce their exposure, and every government, business, and institution can take targeted actions that improve conditions for everyone. This dashboard exists to give people that information clearly, visually, and personally — so that a person in Lagos, Abuja, or Warri can look at their phone, understand their environment in thirty seconds, and make better decisions for themselves and their families today.
